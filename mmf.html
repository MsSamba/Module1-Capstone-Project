Money Market Funds

What is a Money Market Fund?

A money market fund is a type of mutual fund that invests in low-risk, short-term debt securities such Treasury bills, commercial paper, and municipal debt. These funds are designed to provide high liquidity and stability, making them suitable for short-term savings or emergency funds.

Pros of investing in MMFs

  • Professionally managed by experienced professionals who actively seek out high-quality, short-term debt instruments. This frees you from the burden of individual security selection.
  • High liquidity making it easy to access your investment when needed at short notice typically, 1 - 3 business days.
  • Relatively low risk compared to stocks or bonds due to short-term nature of the underlying investments in money market funds.

Money Market Funds can be a good fit for investors seeking:

  • A safe and low-risk investment option to park their money.
  • Easy access to their funds while still earning a modest return.
  • A place to temporarily save funds for a capital intense project or investing in higher-risk, higher-return options.
