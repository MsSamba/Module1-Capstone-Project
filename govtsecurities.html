Government Securities

What are Government Securities?

Government Securities are debt instruments offered by the Kenyan Government in form of Treasury Bills (T-Bills) and Treasury Bonds (T-Bonds). In simple terms, it is loaning the government money and it will repay with interest.


T-Bills and Bonds are considered to be among of the safest investment options in Kenya because they are backed by the governmentâ€™s full faith and credit.


The Central Bank of Kenya (CBK) is the intermediary that deals with Treasury Bills and Bonds.

Comparison Between T-Bills and T-Bonds

T-Bills T-Bonds
Maturity Short-term, typically 91, 182, or 364 days. Medium to long-term, ranging from 1 to 30 years.
Minimum Investment Amount KES. 100,000 KES. 50,000
Interest Sold at a discount, meaning investors receive the face value at maturity, with the difference being the interest earned. Pay regular interest payments (usually semi-annually) until maturity.
Purpose Used by the government to raise short-term funds. Used by the government to raise medium to long-term funds.
Trading Not typically traded on the secondary market. Can be traded on the secondary market.
Risk Generally considered low-risk due to government backing. Generally considered low-risk due to government backing.

Treasury Bills are suitable for investors seeking:

  • Safe and secure investment of their capital.
  • Regular income generation over short-term periods.
  • A parking place for their funds before investing in higher-risk, higher-return options.

Treasury Bonds are suitable for investors seeking:

  • Safe and secure investment of their capital.
  • Regular income generation over medium-term to long-term periods.
  • A parking place for their funds before investing in higher-risk, higher-return options.
