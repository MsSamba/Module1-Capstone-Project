SACCOs: Building Wealth Together

What are SACCOs?

Saving and Credit Cooperatives, commonly known as SACCOs, offer a unique and member-driven approach to saving and borrowing money. Unlike traditional banks, SACCOs are cooperative societies owned and operated by their members. This member-centric structure fosters a sense of community and shared goals.


SACCOs bring together people with a common bond, such as working for the same employer, living in the same geographical area, or belonging to the same religious group. Members pool their savings and access loans at competitive interest rates. SACCOs operate on the principle of cooperation, with members playing an active role in decision-making processes.

Benefits of Saving in a SACCO

  • Competitive Interest Rates: SACCOs often offer competitive interest rates on savings compared to traditional banks.
  • Flexible Savings Options: Many SACCOs offer various savings accounts with different features and benefits to cater to diverse member needs.
  • Sense of Community: Being in a SACCO fosters a sense of community and shared goals among members.
  • Dividend Distribution: Some SACCOs distribute dividends to members based on their savings and the SACCO’s performance.

Benefits of Borrowing from SACCOs

  • Favorable Loan Terms: SACCOs may offer loan terms with lower interest rates and more flexible repayment structures compared to commercial banks, especially for smaller loan amounts.
  • Understanding of Members’ Needs: SACCOs often have a better understanding of their members’ financial situations, which can lead to more flexible loan considerations.
  • Focus on Member Development: Some SACCOs offer financial literacy programs and workshops to empower members to make informed financial decisions.

SACCOs can be a good fit for individuals seeking:

  • A supportive community to save and borrow money.
  • Competitive interest rates on savings and potentially favorable loan terms.
  • The opportunity to participate in a member-owned and democratically controlled financial institution.

It’s important to note that SACCOs may have limitations compared to traditional banks, such as fewer branch networks and potentially lower deposit insurance coverage.
However, for those seeking a cooperative and community-oriented approach to managing their finances, SACCOs can be a powerful tool for financial growth.
